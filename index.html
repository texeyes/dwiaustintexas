After a DWI arrest in Texas, you have exactly 15 days to request an Administrative License Revocation (ALR) hearing with Texas DPS. Miss this window and your license is automatically suspended — no exceptions. Call an attorney today.

Day 1 — Arrest Date
The 15-Day Clock Starts

From the moment of arrest, you have 15 calendar days to request an ALR hearing. Your license will be confiscated and a temporary driving permit issued. Contact an attorney immediately.

Days 1–15 — Critical Window
ALR Hearing Request Deadline

Your attorney files the ALR hearing request with Texas DPS. This buys time and creates an early opportunity to question the arresting officer under oath — valuable intel for your criminal defense.

Days 30–90
Case Filed & Arraignment

The Travis County DA's office files the criminal charge. You appear in court for arraignment, enter a not guilty plea, and your attorney begins discovery — requesting dashcam footage, blood test results, and officer reports.

Months 2–6
Negotiations & Pre-Trial Motions

Your attorney reviews all evidence and may file motions to suppress. Many cases are reduced or dismissed at this stage — a good attorney identifies weaknesses in the stop, the test, or chain of custody before trial.

Months 6–18
Resolution or Trial

Most Austin DWI cases resolve without trial — through dismissal, deferred adjudication, or a plea to a lesser charge. If necessary, your attorney takes the case to a Travis County jury.

Common Questions

Austin DWI FAQ

Answers to the questions most people have in the hours after a DWI arrest in Austin, Texas.

```
Can a DWI be dismissed in Texas?
Yes. Dismissals happen when an attorney successfully challenges the legality of the traffic stop, demonstrates flaws in the breathalyzer or blood test, or proves the field sobriety test was improperly administered. Experienced DWI attorneys in Austin secure dismissals regularly.
What is an ALR hearing and why does it matter?
An Administrative License Revocation hearing is a separate civil proceeding where you contest the automatic suspension of your Texas driver's license. You must request it within 15 days of arrest. Beyond saving your license, the ALR hearing lets your attorney depose the arresting officer early — often revealing weaknesses in the state's case.
Will a DWI stay on my record permanently in Texas?
For most adult convictions, yes. Texas does not allow expunction of DWI convictions. However, if your case is dismissed or you receive deferred adjudication (in limited circumstances), you may be eligible for expunction or a non-disclosure order. This is a critical reason to fight your charge rather than simply plead guilty.
What if I refused the breathalyzer?
Refusing a breath or blood test in Texas triggers an automatic 180-day license suspension under implied consent laws — longer than the 90-day suspension for a failed test. However, refusal also means the prosecution has less direct BAC evidence, which an attorney can use strategically in your defense.
How much does a DWI attorney in Austin cost?
Austin DWI attorney fees typically range from $2,500 to $10,000+ depending on case complexity, prior history, and whether it goes to trial. Consider this against the total cost of a conviction — fines, surcharges, insurance increases, and career impact often exceed $17,000 over three years. Many firms offer payment plans.
Can I drive after a DWI arrest?
When you're arrested for DWI in Texas, your license is typically confiscated and you receive a temporary paper permit that allows driving for 40 days. After that, if no ALR hearing was requested, your license suspends automatically. If your attorney requests the ALR hearing, driving privileges often continue until the hearing concludes.
```
